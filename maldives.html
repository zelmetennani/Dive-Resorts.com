Diving in the Maldives

The Maldives is a dream destination for divers, offering crystal clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and an incredible diversity of marine life. With over 1,000 coral islands spread across 26 atolls, the diving opportunities are virtually endless.

From manta ray cleaning stations to shark-filled channels and vibrant coral gardens, the Maldives offers some of the most spectacular underwater experiences on the planet.

Best Time to Visit

Year-round, with peak season from November to April

Dive Experience

All levels, though some sites have strong currents

Marine Life

Manta rays, whale sharks, reef sharks, turtles, and over 2,000 species of fish

Popular Dive Sites in the Maldives

Manta Point

Famous cleaning station where manta rays gather to be cleaned by smaller fish.

Depth: 10-30m | Level: All levels

Fish Head (Mushimasmingili Thila)

Renowned for its grey reef sharks and abundant fish life.

Depth: 10-35m | Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Hanifaru Bay

UNESCO Biosphere Reserve famous for manta ray and whale shark feeding aggregations.

Depth: 5-15m | Level: Snorkeling and Beginner

Maaya Thila

One of the most famous dive sites in the Maldives, known for its white tip reef sharks.

Depth: 6-30m | Level: Intermediate to Advanced
